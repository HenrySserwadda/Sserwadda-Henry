THE TOPIC
     In katogo zone, kinawataka, a densely populated suburb in Nakawa division, Kampala, access to clean water and piped water is a significantly a challenge.
     Many residents rely heavily on unprotected water sources such as open shallow wells, protected springs to surface run off contamination, and broken water channels. Because of the high-water table and poor sanitation infrastructure(such as poorly constructed pit la-trines) in this formal settlement, these water sources are highly vulnerable to contamina-tion.
Therefore, this study seeks to investigate how relying on these unprotected water sources directly influences the prevalence and recurring spikes of water borne diseases like chol-era, typhoid, and bacillary dysentery among the katogo community.
